# SCAMPER Method

## Strategy

Apply each of the seven lenses to the subject in turn. For each lens, generate at least one concrete idea — even if it seems impractical: **S**ubstitute (what could replace a component?), **C**ombine (what could be merged?), **A**dapt (what could be borrowed from elsewhere?), **M**odify/Magnify (what could be changed or amplified?), **P**ut to other use (what else could this serve?), **E**liminate (what could be removed?), **R**everse/Rearrange (what happens if the order or direction is flipped?). Collect the viable ideas and return the top 3 recommendations.

## When to apply

- Product or process strategy sessions where incremental improvements feel exhausted.
- Rethinking an existing workflow, structure, or document format.
- Generating options when the user is stuck in "one obvious way" thinking.
- Innovation sprints or strategic offsite preparation.

## Trade-offs

- **Pro**: Systematic — covers creative angles the user wouldn't naturally reach.
- **Pro**: Fast to execute; produces tangible ideas, not abstract principles.
- **Con**: Some lenses will feel forced for a given context — that's fine, skip quickly.
- **Con**: Ideas are starting points, not finished solutions — requires follow-up refinement.
