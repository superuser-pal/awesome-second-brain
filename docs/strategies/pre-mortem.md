# Pre-mortem Analysis

## Strategy

Assume it is now 6 months in the future and the plan has failed completely. Describe the most plausible failure scenario in detail. Work backwards: what chain of events led to this outcome? Identify the 3–5 highest-probability causes. For each cause, propose a concrete mitigation. Return the failure narrative, root causes, and mitigations.

## When to apply

- Project kick-off before committing to a plan of action.
- OKR or goal-setting reviews where risk assessment is missing.
- Before a major decision, launch, or irreversible commitment.
- Weekly planning when a key initiative is starting the following week.

## Trade-offs

- **Pro**: Surfaces risks that optimism bias suppresses — especially useful in planning sessions.
- **Pro**: Produces actionable mitigations, not just a list of worries.
- **Con**: Can feel pessimistic if the team is already cautious — calibrate framing accordingly.
- **Con**: May over-weight dramatic failure modes and under-weight slow-burn risks.
