# 5 Whys Deep Dive

## Strategy

Given the problem or symptom, ask "Why does this happen?" Record the answer. Ask "Why?" again about that answer. Repeat until the root cause is reached (typically 3–6 iterations). Do not suggest solutions until the root cause is clearly named. Return the full why-chain and the identified root cause.

## When to apply

- Retrospectives and week-close reviews where something went wrong.
- Note analysis where a pattern keeps recurring and the source is unclear.
- Debugging a decision or plan that produced unexpected results.
- Any time the user says "this keeps happening" or "I don't know why X failed."

## Trade-offs

- **Pro**: Simple and fast — surfaces root causes without complex frameworks.
- **Pro**: Keeps analysis focused on causes, not symptoms.
- **Con**: Can lead to a single causal chain and miss parallel contributing factors.
- **Con**: Effectiveness depends on the accuracy of each "why" answer — garbage in, garbage out.
