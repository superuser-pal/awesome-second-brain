# Self-Refinement

## Strategy

Provide an initial concise answer, critique it briefly, and refine if necessary.

## When to apply

- Writing and editing tasks where a first draft + targeted improvement is more efficient than a blank-slate critique loop.
- Situations where the initial answer is likely good but could benefit from one targeted pass.
- Lighter-weight alternative to Reflexion when a full critique cycle feels excessive.

## Trade-offs

- **Pro**: Lighter than Reflexion — refinement only happens when the critique finds something actionable.
- **Pro**: Produces a polished output with one pass.
- **Con**: "Only if necessary" is subjective — the model may under-refine or over-refine.
- **Con**: Less transparent than Reflexion (critique may be omitted from final output).
