# Red Team vs Blue Team

## Strategy

Split the analysis into two roles. **Red Team (attacker)**: assume the plan will be challenged, exploited, or undermined — find the 3 most damaging vulnerabilities or objections. **Blue Team (defender)**: respond to each Red Team challenge with a concrete hardening measure or counter-argument. After the exchange, summarise which vulnerabilities remain unresolved and what the net-strengthened plan looks like.

## When to apply

- Strategy reviews before presenting a plan to critical stakeholders.
- Any plan that has been built collaboratively and may have blind spots from groupthink.
- Proposal writing where the user needs to anticipate pushback.
- Decision-making where the downside risk justifies adversarial scrutiny.

## Trade-offs

- **Pro**: Finds weaknesses before they are found by adversaries or circumstances.
- **Pro**: The defence step makes the final output stronger, not just more worried.
- **Con**: Can feel combative — frame as "stress-testing" rather than "attacking" in collaborative contexts.
- **Con**: The Red Team's imagination limits the attack surface; unknown unknowns remain hidden.
