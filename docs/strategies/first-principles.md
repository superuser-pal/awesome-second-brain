# First Principles Analysis

## Strategy

Identify every assumption embedded in the problem. Challenge each one: is this actually true, or is it inherited convention? Once stripped bare, reconstruct the answer from the ground up using only what can be verified. Return the rebuilt understanding alongside the final answer.

## When to apply

- Strategic decisions where the "obvious" solution is being challenged or feels stale.
- Synthesising notes where inherited framing may be distorting conclusions.
- Refactoring plans or workflows that have grown organically and may have inefficiencies baked in.
- Any situation where the user says "why do we even do it this way?"

## Trade-offs

- **Pro**: Breaks out of path-dependency — finds genuinely novel solutions rather than incremental tweaks.
- **Pro**: Makes hidden assumptions explicit and auditable.
- **Con**: Time-intensive — inappropriate for time-sensitive or low-stakes questions.
- **Con**: Can produce impractical answers if real-world constraints are stripped too aggressively.
