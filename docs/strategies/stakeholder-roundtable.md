# Stakeholder Round Table

## Strategy

Identify 3–5 relevant stakeholder personas for the problem at hand (e.g. end user, budget holder, technical lead, ops team, future-maintainer). Give each a brief role and a distinct interest. Have each persona present their perspective, concern, or requirement in 2–3 sentences. Facilitate a synthesis that acknowledges the tensions and proposes the most balanced resolution. Label each speaker clearly.

## When to apply

- Requirements gathering where multiple parties have conflicting needs.
- Decisions that will affect more than one team, user type, or system.
- Note analysis where the source material reflects multiple viewpoints.
- Any complex decision where single-perspective thinking is a risk.

## Trade-offs

- **Pro**: Surfaces blind spots that emerge only from other roles or incentive structures.
- **Pro**: The synthesis step forces honest trade-off acknowledgement rather than false consensus.
- **Con**: Persona quality depends on how well their interests are defined — shallow personas produce shallow input.
- **Con**: Can feel theatrical for simple decisions; reserve for genuinely multi-stakeholder problems.
