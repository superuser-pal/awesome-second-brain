# 09 — Recommended Plugins

> Obsidian is powerful on its own, but these plugins make ASB feel like a superpower.

---

## 🛠️ The "Big Three" Essentials

If you only install a few plugins, make it these. They are the engine that powers the ASB experience.

### 1. Tasks

- **What it does**: Gathers every `- [ ]` checkbox from every file in your vault.
- **Why it matters**: ASB often creates tasks for you while you're brainstorming. Instead of hunting through folders to find them, the Tasks plugin lets you see _everything_ you need to do in one central view.

### 2. Dataview

- **What it does**: Turns your notes into a searchable database.
- **Why it matters**: Every note ASB creates has "Frontmatter" (labels like `domain`, `status`, and `project`). Dataview lets you build automatic dashboards like "Show me every project I haven't touched in a week" or "List all books I've read this year."

### 3. Calendar

- **What it does**: Adds a simple calendar view to your sidebar.
- **Why it matters**: Since ASB logs all your work sessions by date, the calendar is the easiest way to jump back in time and see what you were working on last Tuesday.

---

## 🚀 Leveling Up Your Workflow

Once you're comfortable, these add some serious polish.

- **Templater**: ASB uses templates for everything (notes, projects, sessions). Templater makes those templates "smart"—automatically filling in the date, the project name, and even your current mood.
- **Excalidraw**: Sometimes words aren't enough. Use this for sketching out ideas or mapping out complex systems. It's essentially a digital whiteboard inside your notes.
- **Terminal (by polyipseity)**: This is for the power users. It puts a command line right inside Obsidian. You can talk to ASB in one pane and watch your notes update in the other. No more switching windows.

---

## ⚙️ Recommended Obsidian Settings

To get the most out of ASB, I recommend making these quick tweaks in your Obsidian settings (`Cmd + ,`):

| Setting             | Recommendation      | Why?                                               |
| ------------------- | ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **New link format** | Wikilinks (`[[ ]]`) | Easiest way to connect notes.                      |
| **New note folder** | `Inbox/Notes/`      | Keeps your vault clean. Process first, file later. |
| **Line numbers**    | ON                  | Makes it easier to point ASB to specific lines.    |
| **Default view**    | Source mode         | Best for seeing the raw structure ASB creates.     |

---

## Final Thoughts

Plugins are great, but don't overdo it. Start with the **Big Three**, get your workflow down, and only add more when you feel a specific "friction" in your day-to-day work.
